# Blog topic: the anatomy of a staff-engineer promotion case

**People-leadership theme.** The vault holds a real, name-bearing staff-engineer promotion
case in its reports folder. The *document* is off-limits (names, specifics, internal
context) — but the **genre** is a strong evergreen post: what actually goes into a
convincing promotion case for the staff+ jump, written from the manager's side.

**The angle** (fully anonymized — no employer, no person):
- What "operating at staff level" evidence looks like vs. senior — scope, blast radius,
  force-multiplication, ambiguity handled.
- How to structure the written case: the narrative, the artifacts/proof, the
  counter-arguments you pre-empt.
- The manager's job before the packet: building the track record over quarters, not
  assembling it the week of calibration.
- Common failure modes (activity ≠ impact; "great engineer" ≠ "staff engineer").

**Why it fits the loop:** it's the cleanest demonstration of the loop's core rule — take a
name-bearing internal artifact and ship only the reusable, anonymized lesson. It also
diversifies the blog away from the AI cluster into engineering-leadership, which is
on-brand (Senior EM voice) and under-served on the site.

**Sanitization (mandatory):** never reference the real person, employer, level names, or any
specific from the source doc. The post is about the *shape* of a staff case, drawn from
general practice — the vault report is only the prompt, never the content.
